Volunteer Experience

For three days - July 29th, 2018 to July 31st, 2018 - I was a volunteer ball kid for a tennis tournament, Mubadala Silicon Valley Classic, hosted at San Jose State University for Women's Tennis Association (WTA) players. Every day, I worked with a different team for about eight hours to help make sure that the match flows as quickly and efficiently as possible and the players have everything they need. In order to be a ball kid, you should have some background knowledge of how a match is played and how the score is kept.

During the last two weeks, from July 9th, 2018 to July 20th, 2018, of my summer swim team, the Dublin Green Gators, I worked as an assistant coach. What I would do for one hour after my practice, is help correct and improve the techniques of any younger swimmers that the coaches decided needed help. To do this, I had to be able to analyze the swimmer, and think of - maybe even create new - drills to help the swimmer. If I could not think of any or the swimmer was just not picking up the skill, I could ask for help from the other assistant coaches.
